"The Last Train"

As the clock struck midnight, Eva stepped onto the platform, clutching a faded letter. The train's whistle echoed through the cold air, its lights cutting through the fog. She hadn't seen Leo in ten years, not since he left without goodbye. The letter, recently found behind an old bookshelf, told of a missed meeting and a love never forgotten. The last train hissed to a stop. The doors opened. Eva held her breath. A man stepped out, older, eyes familiar. “Eva?” he whispered. She smiled, tears glistening. "You’re late," she said. He took her hand. “But I’m here.”
